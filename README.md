1. Principle of Verified Trust (Zero Trust Architecture)
Since employees use personal devices and work from various locations, the business must move away from perimeter-based security.
Action: Implement a policy where every access request is fully authenticated, authorized, and encrypted. No device is trusted by default, whether it is in the office or a cafe.
Mitigation: This addresses the risk of unmanaged personal computers by ensuring only 'healthy' devices (those with active antivirus and encryption) can connect to company resources.
2. Principle of Secure Connectivity and Identity Assurance
Working from public Wi-Fi increases the risk of interception, and phishing remains a primary threat to credentials.
Action: Mandate the use of a corporate VPN for all remote work and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all accounts.
Mitigation: MFA provides a critical safety net; even if an employee is tricked by a phishing email into revealing their password, the attacker cannot gain access without the second factor.
3. Principle of Psychological Safety and Resilience
The current culture of fear regarding phishing is counterproductive, as it discourages incident reporting.
Action: Establish a "No-Blame" reporting culture. Replace punitive measures (like firing) with constructive, gamified security awareness training.
Mitigation: By removing the fear of termination, employees are more likely to report suspicious activity or accidental clicks immediately, allowing for faster containment of breaches.
4. Principle of Data Centricity and Least Privilege
In a consultancy, data is the most valuable asset. Security should follow the data, not just the network.
Action: Implement the "Principle of Least Privilege" (PoLP), ensuring staff only have access to the specific data required for their current projects. Use cloud-based Data Loss Prevention (DLP) tools to monitor sensitive information.
Mitigation: This limits the "blast radius" of a potential breach; if one account is compromised, the attacker cannot access the entire company database.
5. Principle of Scalable Oversight (Managed Security)
Without an on-site IT technician, the business lacks professional monitoring and incident response capabilities.
Action: Outsource security monitoring to a Managed Service Provider (MSP) or utilize automated Cloud Security Posture Management (CSPM) tools.
Mitigation: This provides 24/7 professional oversight and automated responses to threats, filling the gap left by the lack of an on-site IT department.
